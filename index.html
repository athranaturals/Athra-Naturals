Busting Myths

Common Misconceptions

In the world of health and wellness, coconut oil often finds itself at the centre of various debates. Here are the facts.

Myth 1Coconut oil is unhealthy because it is high in saturated fat
Fact
While coconut oil contains saturated fat, it is primarily composed of medium-chain triglycerides (MCTs) that are metabolized differently than long-chain fats found in animal products. In moderation, it can be a healthy addition to your diet.
Myth 2Virgin coconut oil is the same as regular coconut oil
Fact
Virgin coconut oil is extracted from fresh coconut meat without heat or chemicals, preserving its nutrients and flavour. Regular coconut oil may be refined and lack these benefits entirely.
Myth 3Coconut oil causes weight gain
Fact
When consumed in moderation, coconut oil can actually support metabolism and energy levels. Like any calorie-dense food, overconsumption may lead to weight gain — but moderate use supports a healthy lifestyle.
Myth 4Coconut oil is only good for cooking
Fact
Beyond the kitchen, coconut oil has numerous applications in skincare, haircare, baby care, and even oral health through oil pulling — making it a truly versatile product in your wellness routine.
Myth 5All coconut oil is the same
Fact
There are various types of coconut oil — virgin, refined, and hydrogenated — each with different properties and uses. Virgin coconut oil is the gold standard, preserving maximum nutrients and natural benefits.

Have Questions?

Frequently Asked Questions

What is fermented virgin coconut oil and how is it different?
Fermented virgin coconut oil is made by extracting fresh coconut milk and allowing it to naturally ferment and separate over 24 to 48 hours at room temperature — no heat, no chemicals. This cold-process method retains maximum natural nutrients, lauric acid, and antioxidants, making it far superior to commercially refined coconut oil.
Is ATHRA NATURALS coconut oil safe for cooking?
Yes, absolutely. Our fermented virgin coconut oil has a natural, mild coconut aroma and is perfect for cooking, sauteing, and baking. It has a moderate smoke point suitable for everyday Indian cooking. Many customers also use it as a finishing oil for salads and smoothies.
How should I use it for hair and skin?
For hair: warm a small amount and massage into the scalp and lengths. Leave for 30 minutes or overnight before washing. For skin: apply a thin layer directly as a moisturiser, massage oil, or makeup remover. Its high lauric acid content gives it natural antibacterial properties, ideal for sensitive skin too.
Why does the oil sometimes solidify?
This is completely natural and a sign of high purity! Virgin coconut oil solidifies below 24 degrees C and melts above it. During cooler months it may turn white and solid — this does not affect quality at all. Simply place the bottle in warm water to liquefy it.
What is the shelf life and how should I store it?
Our coconut oil has a shelf life of 18 to 24 months when stored in a cool, dry place away from direct sunlight. Always use a clean, dry spoon to avoid moisture contamination and extend its natural life.
